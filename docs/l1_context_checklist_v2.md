# Layer 1 Context Checklist (v2)

Tick only what is clearly observable. Do not score assumptions.

Scoring guide per column: `0-4 weak`, `5-8 moderate`, `9+ strong`.

Use totals as weak directional signals only. Observable behaviour outweighs score totals.

## 🟢 Proceed

- Stakeholders independently describe the same issue.
- Low disagreement about the core problem.
- Decisions are rarely reopened.
- Frontline experience matches the data trend.
- Outcomes are predictable for similar inputs.
- Previous interventions produced repeatable results.
- Movement from decision to execution is fast.
- Teams already work in broadly similar ways.
- Shared success criteria are visible.
- Minimal escalation after decisions.
- Ownership and accountability are clear.
- Confidence in direction is high.

## 🟡 Align

- Different stakeholders define different problems.
- The same issue is repeatedly reopened.
- Decisions are interpreted differently by team or region.
- "Yes, but" behaviour appears after formal agreement.
- Execution diverges across groups.
- Progress depends heavily on a specific leader.
- Side conversations continue after decisions.
- Escalations persist despite alignment meetings.
- Incentives or KPIs pull teams in different directions.
- Success measures are not shared.
- Rework cycles repeat.
- Teams challenge the framing itself.

## 🔍 Probe

- Data signals are contradictory.
- Same action produces different outcomes.
- Cause-effect is unclear.
- Experts disagree with plausible explanations.
- "It depends" is a recurring answer.
- Judgement is replacing shared evidence.
- Outcomes are highly variable.
- Root-cause story keeps changing.
- Experiments are absent, weak, or inconclusive.
- Hidden variables are likely present.
- Direction changes frequently.
- Confidence in explanation is low.

## ⛔ Stop

- Leadership attention is low or inconsistent.
- Work continues out of habit.
- Improvement effort has little visible effect.
- Ownership is unclear or unstable.
- Value is hard to explain.
- Effort outweighs benefit.
- Work is frequently deprioritised.
- Language reflects obligation, not purpose.
- Capability overlaps with other work.
- Outputs are rarely used.
- Customer impact is unclear.
- Opportunity cost is visible.

## Decision rule

1. Identify the single strongest observable pattern first.
2. Count ticks per column as a secondary sense-check.
3. Select the highest column as dominant context.
4. If top two columns are within 2 ticks, treat as mixed.
5. Before concluding `Align`, run an incentive-conflict check:
   - Are people disagreeing on the problem?
   - Or are they aligned but rewarded to behave differently?
6. Keep Layer 1 and Layer 2 separate:
   - Layer 1 decides permission/timing (🟢 `Proceed`, 🟡 `Align`, 🔍 `Probe`, ⛔ `Stop`).
   - Layer 2 decides action type (🔧 `Stabilise`, 📐 `Rationalise`, 🚀 `Optimise`).
7. If mixed, take a bounded next step rather than full rollout.
