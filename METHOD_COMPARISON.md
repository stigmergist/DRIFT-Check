# METHOD COMPARISON

This page compares DRIFT Check with common frameworks, methods, and approaches used to understand organisational situations and guide action.

DRIFT Check is not a replacement for these approaches. It is a thinking prompt that helps you test fit between situation, capability state, and action type before committing effort.

## Strategy

| Framework / method | Primary purpose | How DRIFT differs | How DRIFT helps with it | Typical risks DRIFT identifies for this framework |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SWOT | Summarise strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats | DRIFT focuses on observable behaviour and action-fit, not static category listing | Turns SWOT points into action timing questions: act now, align, probe, or stop | Treating a SWOT list as a decision; jumping to action without checking capability readiness |
| PESTLE | Scan external macro factors | DRIFT links external signals to internal capability reality and practical next action | Helps distinguish external pressure from internal misalignment or instability | Overreacting to external trends while core delivery capability is unstable |
| Balanced Scorecard | Track performance across strategic dimensions | DRIFT is diagnostic-thinking support, not a KPI framework | Interprets score movement through context and capability state before interventions | Mistaking metric movement for real capability progress |
| OKRs | Set goals and align effort around measurable outcomes | DRIFT tests whether system conditions can support the target before scaling effort | Helps test if poor progress is target quality, alignment, or execution-state issue | Setting ambitious goals on misaligned or unreliable capability foundations |
| Wardley Mapping | Map value chains and evolution to inform strategic plays | DRIFT is less about market positioning and more about internal context/action fit | Helps test whether a strategic play is executable given current capability reality | Choosing strong strategic plays that fail because execution capability is unstable or misaligned |
| TOGAF | Design and govern enterprise architecture and transformation roadmaps | DRIFT is state-and-fit oriented, not architecture-domain structured | Tests whether architecture decisions match actual delivery stability and organisational alignment | Producing target-state architectures that outpace current capability readiness |

## Change / Programmes

| Framework / method | Primary purpose | How DRIFT differs | How DRIFT helps with it | Typical risks DRIFT identifies for this framework |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MSP (Managing Successful Programmes) | Govern and deliver coordinated change via programme management disciplines | DRIFT tests programme assumptions against observed context and capability conditions | Helps identify when programme controls are strong but shared understanding or value is weak | Programme momentum continuing despite unclear value or persistent misalignment |
| PRINCE2 | Structured project governance and control framework | DRIFT focuses on fit of action to observed state rather than control process compliance | Improves stage-gate decisions by checking readiness, value, and capability reliability | Well-run governance cycles driving delivery of the wrong or low-value thing |
| PMBOK | General project management body of knowledge for planning and delivery | DRIFT checks whether project methods match situational readiness and capability state | Improves project initiation and replanning by surfacing misfit early | Strong project plans built on weak alignment or unstable execution conditions |
| SAFe / scaled agile frameworks | Coordinate agile delivery across multiple teams and portfolios | DRIFT is method-agnostic and evaluates whether scaling is appropriate now | Helps test whether teams should align or stabilise before scaling cadence and governance | Scaling delivery rituals across fragmented understanding or unstable practices |
| Agile (team-level, non-scaled) | Deliver value iteratively through small batches, feedback, and adaptation | DRIFT does not prescribe ceremonies; it checks whether agile practices match situational needs | Helps teams distinguish between learning-mode and performance-mode work before choosing cadence | Agile ritual compliance without real improvement in outcome reliability or clarity |
| Kotter 8-Step Change | Lead organisational change through staged mobilisation | DRIFT evaluates whether shared understanding exists before mobilisation and scaling | Helps detect Align/Probe conditions before launching broad change activity | Launching large change when understanding is fragmented or value is unclear |
| ADKAR | Manage individual change adoption (awareness, desire, knowledge, ability, reinforcement) | DRIFT is system-state oriented; ADKAR is people adoption oriented | Helps separate adoption issues from deeper process/capability instability | Framing structural capability problems as only people-resistance problems |

## Risk / Governance

| Framework / method | Primary purpose | How DRIFT differs | How DRIFT helps with it | Typical risks DRIFT identifies for this framework |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ERM (Enterprise Risk Management) | Manage strategic and operational risk across the enterprise | DRIFT surfaces live misfit risks from behaviour and outcomes, not only catalogued risks | Adds dynamic risk sensing through observable signal checks across context and capability | Risk frameworks that are complete on paper but miss emerging execution drift |
| Risk registers / risk matrices | Identify, score, and track known risks | DRIFT focuses on observed emerging misfit, not only pre-listed risks | Surfaces hidden systemic risks from action-state mismatch early | Treating risk logs as complete while weak signals show capability drift |
| COBIT | Govern and control enterprise IT for value, risk, and compliance | DRIFT adds practical situational interpretation beneath control objectives | Helps connect governance controls to observable delivery behaviour and value flow | Compliance-success narratives while service performance remains inconsistent |
| CMMI / maturity models | Assess process maturity and target higher maturity levels | DRIFT does not assume maturity progression equals value progression | Helps verify whether maturity work maps to actual outcomes and usage | Chasing maturity scores while core outcomes remain inconsistent |
| EFQM (often written EQFM) | Assess organisational excellence and improvement maturity holistically | DRIFT prioritises immediate action-state fit over broad excellence scoring constructs | Helps convert assessment insights into sequenced actions that match current system state | Excellence scoring activity without corresponding improvement in reliable outcomes |

## Delivery / Improvement

| Framework / method | Primary purpose | How DRIFT differs | How DRIFT helps with it | Typical risks DRIFT identifies for this framework |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cynefin | Classify context type (clear/complicated/complex/chaotic) and response style | DRIFT uses simpler operational cues around context readiness and action-fit sequence | Helps translate context classification into stabilise/rationalise/optimise action decisions | Misclassifying context and applying the wrong response pattern |
| OODA Loop | Improve decision speed through observe-orient-decide-act cycles | DRIFT prioritises fit and clarity over speed alone | Strengthens Observe/Orient quality by forcing signal quality and misfit checks | Fast cycles that amplify wrong assumptions or unstable process patterns |
| JFDI / "just do it" execution | Move quickly by acting immediately and learning from results | DRIFT adds explicit fit and readiness checks before action | Helps preserve execution speed while reducing avoidable misfit and rework | Fast action on unclear-value or unstable systems that creates compounding damage |
| PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) | Continuous improvement through iterative learning loops | DRIFT adds explicit pre-checks on whether the system is ready for improvement work | Helps decide whether to align or probe before launching improvement cycles | Running improvement loops on unclear-value or uncontrolled capabilities |
| Design Thinking | Explore user needs and iterate solution ideas through testing | DRIFT focuses less on solution ideation and more on capability/context fit for action | Helps test whether promising concepts can be delivered reliably in current system conditions | Strong concept discovery followed by weak execution due to unstable capability foundations |
| Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) | Understand customer progress needs to guide product/service choices | DRIFT complements JTBD by testing operational readiness to deliver against discovered jobs | Helps connect customer insight to practical action sequencing and system constraints | Insight-rich opportunity maps with poor real-world delivery performance |
| Lean / Kaizen | Reduce waste and improve flow continuously | DRIFT checks whether simplification is appropriate at the current capability state | Prevents premature simplification by sequencing stabilise before streamline | Removing buffers from unstable systems and increasing fragility |
| Six Sigma / DMAIC | Reduce variation and improve quality with structured analysis | DRIFT starts from observed state and fit-for-action before deep analytical effort | Helps scope DMAIC to problems where value and control conditions are present | High analysis effort applied to low-value or structurally misaligned problems |
| Theory of Constraints (TOC) | Improve throughput by identifying and managing bottlenecks | DRIFT broadens from single constraint logic to context/action/capability interaction | Tests whether the identified constraint is true bottleneck vs symptom of misalignment | Local optimisation of a bottleneck that worsens wider system behaviour |
| ITIL | Standardise and improve IT service management practices | DRIFT evaluates whether process standardisation matches real operational stability | Helps decide when to stabilise operations before additional process optimisation | Process formalisation that masks unresolved variability and recurring incidents |

## Practical use

Use this table as a translation aid:

1. Start with the framework your team already uses.
2. Run DRIFT Check on the same situation.
3. Compare where the framework recommends action vs where DRIFT indicates readiness and fit.
4. If they conflict, investigate the conflict before scaling effort.

## Technique fit matrix (Context x Capability)

Rows show context state (Proceed, Align, Probe, Stop). Columns show capability action need (Stabilise, Rationalise, Optimise). Each cell lists techniques DRIFT would typically treat as most appropriate to apply first.

| Context state \ Capability action need | Stabilise | Rationalise | Optimise |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proceed | - ITIL incident/problem controls<br>- PDCA<br>- OODA<br>- targeted risk register updates | - Lean/Kaizen<br>- TOC<br>- Agile retrospectives<br>- PMBOK/PRINCE2 process simplification | - OKRs<br>- Balanced Scorecard tuning<br>- Six Sigma optimisation<br>- TOGAF architecture refinement |
| Align | - Facilitation-first ADKAR/Kotter alignment work<br>- stakeholder mapping<br>- risk framing before delivery changes | - Team-level Agile ceremonies for shared understanding<br>- Lean value-stream alignment<br>- programme-level MSP alignment resets | - **Limited optimisation only after alignment signals improve**<br>- Light OKR reset<br>- scorecard recalibration |
| Probe | - Cynefin safe-to-fail probes<br>- OODA learning loops<br>- small JFDI experiments<br>- short PDCA cycles | - Design Thinking experiments<br>- JTBD hypothesis tests<br>- constrained Lean trials | - **Defer heavy optimisation**<br>- **Run micro-optimisations only where reliability is already proven** |
| Stop | - Value challenge<br>- ERM downside review<br>- risk-register closure/containment<br>- governance decision gate (PRINCE2/MSP) | - **Minimal rationalisation focused on exit/simplification**<br>- Remove redundant process overhead | - **Do not optimise**<br>- **Reassess purpose, ownership, and whether work should pause or end** |

Notes for use:

- Treat this as directional guidance, not rigid rules.
- If context and capability signals conflict, prioritise alignment and probing before large optimisation effort.
- Re-check fit frequently as new evidence appears.

## Notes

- This comparison is directional, not exhaustive.
- DRIFT Check is most useful when teams are moving fast, signals are mixed, or actions seem to be producing inconsistent outcomes.
