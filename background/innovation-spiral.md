# The Innovation Spiral

Innovation can be thought of a spiral that requires you to go through complete turns fo assessing if a solution is feasible, viable and desirable.

It is a spiral because at the outside of the spiral you rapidly and shallowly assess these factors broadly and slowly. As you find that there is positive feasability, vaiability and deisarbility, you put further effort in to examining solutoins from those perspectives. The evaluation rate of each increases, and visually you start moving in towards the cetnre of the spiral which is a delivered system.

As you progress you spiral in towards delivering a working solution that delivers for the customers and the business.

You can think of it being a constant sequence of F->V->D->V->D, but it can move in any sequence.

You can think of there being other states to assess between the three primary factors:
- between Feasable and Viable is Sustainable
- between Feasible and Desirable is Usable
- between Viable and Desireable is Valuable

The outisde of the sprial is called different things depending on what is being focussed on first:
- feasible = prototyping (is it even possible?)
- desirable - product/market testing (do people want a solution?)
- viable - business case (if somehting like this existed could we afford to do it?)

Sustainable/Valuable/Usable are only worht assess once you have a first answer to each of Feasible/Viable/Desirable.

If any of them conclude "NO" then STOP.

In the outer loops "NO/STOP" is as valuable as "YES/CONTINUE". As you progress towards delivery you cross a bound where only "YES" is valuable unless somehting is really wrong in your original foundation thinking/assessment. Think of it like water swirlling down a plug-hole, there's an invitable point of interia.
