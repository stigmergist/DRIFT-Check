# Local Optimisation

Local optimisation is improvement in one part of the system that degrades the wider system.

This is a common organisational trap because local metrics often improve quickly and visibly. The damage appears elsewhere and later: extra coordination, hidden fragility, delayed downstream outcomes, or weaker overall value flow.

DRIFT treats local optimisation as a system-level misfit. The action may be well-executed locally and still wrong for the capability stack as a whole.

The antidote is to evaluate interventions at system level. Did value flow improve across boundaries, or did one unit improve by exporting cost and risk to others?
