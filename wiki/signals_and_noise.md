# Signals and Noise

Signal is repeatable evidence that helps explain system behaviour. Noise is variation that looks meaningful but does not reliably predict what happens next.

Most organisational systems produce both. The challenge is not eliminating noise; it is avoiding decisions that over-weight it.

DRIFT favours repeatable patterns over isolated events. A single incident may matter, but recurring patterns usually carry more weight. This is why observation quality is central. If you misread signal and noise, misfit grows.

Risk work also needs two signal types. Known signals track expected failure patterns in stable domains. Discovery signals track anomalies and novelty in shifting domains. Using one signal type for both can hide emerging risk.

Signal quality also depends on timing. In fast-moving environments, stale signal can become misleading even when it was once accurate.
