# Agency

Agency is the ability to change the system or influence its behaviour.

A problem without agency is not a system to optimise. It is a constraint to navigate. This distinction prevents wasted effort. Teams often spend energy trying to improve conditions they do not control, then interpret lack of movement as execution failure.

Agency can be absolute or partial. Partial agency still matters. It allows local adaptation, buffering, and influence over adjacent decisions. But it should be named explicitly so action is scoped to what can actually move.

Good DRIFT practice makes agency visible before intervention planning. When [programme.md](programme.md) work is planned, agency boundaries should be tested early. Where agency is limited, the work becomes constraint navigation rather than system improvement.
