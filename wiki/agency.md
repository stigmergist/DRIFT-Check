# Agency

Agency is the ability to change the system or influence its behaviour.

A problem without agency is not a system to optimise.
It is a constraint to navigate.
This distinction prevents wasted effort.
Teams often spend effort on conditions they do not control, then misread stalled progress as execution failure.

Agency can be absolute or partial.
Partial agency still matters.
It allows local adaptation, buffering, and influence over adjacent decisions.
Name it explicitly so action stays scoped to what can actually move.

Good DRIFT practice makes agency visible before intervention planning.
When [programme.md](programme.md) work begins, test agency boundaries early.
Where agency is limited, the work is constraint navigation, not system improvement.
