# Optimise

Optimise means improving performance, cost, and efficiency of a capability that is already reliable.

Optimisation is powerful and often overused. It works well when the system is stable, understood, and producing consistent outcomes. In that condition, refinement can produce meaningful gains.

When applied too early, optimisation amplifies **[instability](state.md)**. It can make weak assumptions run faster, **[scale](scaling.md)** **[fragile](fragility.md)** dependencies, and hide structural problems behind temporary performance improvements.

The right question before optimisation is simple: are outcomes dependable enough that improvement efforts are acting on signal rather than noise?

Also test for [capability_ceiling](capability_ceiling.md). If performance tuning gives diminishing system-level returns, further optimisation may be the wrong move.
