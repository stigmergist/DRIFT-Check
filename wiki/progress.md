# Progress

Progress is improvement in the system's ability to produce outcomes reliably and efficiently.

Progress is not movement. It is not activity. It is not the volume of work completed. Those can all increase while capability quality stays flat or declines. In DRIFT terms, progress means the system is becoming more dependable and more effective at producing the outcomes that matter.

This is why progress should be judged through behaviour and results over time, not reporting cadence. If outcomes still vary, if issues keep returning, or if success still depends on exceptional effort, progress is weak even when delivery looks busy.

Progress can also be local or systemic. Local progress improves a part of the system. Systemic progress improves value flow across the stack. Confusing the two leads to overclaiming. A local gain that increases friction elsewhere is not system progress.

Progress and value are connected but distinct. A team can make real progress on capability quality while creating little value if it is improving a low-relevance area. A team can also create temporary value without durable progress by sustaining output through heroics.
