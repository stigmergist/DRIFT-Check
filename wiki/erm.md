# ERM

ERM stands for Enterprise Risk Management.

In DRIFT terms, ERM is a governance container for risk visibility, prioritisation, and accountability across the organisation. It helps leadership compare exposure, track actions, and assign ownership.

## What ERM is good at

ERM is strongest when risk is stable enough to define clearly.

It works well for:
- standard taxonomies
- consistent reporting cycles
- control tracking and assurance
- portfolio-level comparability

In plain terms: ERM is strong when the risk picture is clear enough to score and monitor consistently.

## Where ERM can mislead

ERM can create false precision when risk is still shifting.

If uncertainty is compressed into fixed scores too early, governance can look mature while understanding remains shallow. This is why DRIFT separates [risk_types](risk_types.md) and asks teams to switch posture between [control_learning](control_learning.md) modes.

## Practical use with DRIFT

Use ERM as structure, not as proof of certainty.

When risk is Type 2:
- keep assumptions explicit
- include narrative evidence and case traces
- track change signals, not only closure metrics

When risk is Type 1:
- use standard control and assurance routines
- focus on repeatability and recurrence reduction

## Decision question

Ask: is this ERM view helping us control known risk, or hiding uncertainty we still need to learn?
