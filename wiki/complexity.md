# Complexity

Complexity in DRIFT terms is when the same outcome is achieved in multiple different ways.

That pattern increases variation in practice, raises coordination cost, and makes the system harder to reason about. Complexity is not automatically bad. Some variety is useful for resilience and local adaptation. The problem is unmanaged variety that adds friction without adding value.

Complexity is often confused with uncertainty. A practical test helps: if outcomes are stable but methods vary, complexity is likely dominant and rationalisation can help. If outcomes are unstable even with similar methods, uncertainty is likely dominant and probing should come first.
