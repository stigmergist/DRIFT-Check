# Fragility

Fragility is when small issues propagate into larger failures.

Fragile systems can look stable in normal conditions and fail abruptly under stress. In DRIFT terms, fragility often grows when organisations optimise speed or efficiency without preserving enough control, slack, or learning capacity.

## How fragility develops: failure propagation

Fragility develops through a predictable sequence. Instability at a lower layer — unreliable tooling, variable processes, personnel changes — creates inconsistent inputs to the layers above. When those inputs are inconsistent, teams create workarounds: manual checks, parallel processes, or informal coordination.

Over time, workarounds become default practice. The system then depends on extra effort to hide instability. If that effort disappears, hidden problems surface at the top as unpredictable delivery, inconsistent results, or customer experience failures.

What looks like a top-layer crisis is usually lower-layer instability that was masked for too long. A missed deadline that "came out of nowhere" often had warning signs weeks earlier in data velocity, defect rates, or incident frequency.

## Common fragility patterns

Fragility often appears through:

**Removal of buffers from unstable systems.** When a capability is still variable, cutting buffers (time, budget, headcount, inventory) can make the system seem faster. It also makes it more fragile. Small disruptions then become critical.

**Concentration of critical knowledge in few individuals.** Key decisions depend on specific people. Turnover or absence immediately impacts outcomes.

**Automation of unreliable processes.** When underlying processes are inconsistent, automating them locks in the variability and amplifies its impact. What could be manually adjusted becomes structural and harder to fix.

**Aggressive efficiency gains on already-thin margins.** When coordination overhead is already high, removing more slack collapses the system's ability to absorb disruption or variation.

Each pattern looks like progress in the moment. Each pattern increases the brittleness that follows.
